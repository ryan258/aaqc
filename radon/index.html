What is Radon?

Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas that is tasteless, odorless, and colorless.

It results in the breaking down of Radium and Uranium in the Earth's soil. In Minnesota it has been estimated that 2 in 5 homes have Radon leves that are above the safety recommendation.

Outdoors the Radon gas dissipates quickly into the open air rendering it harmless. Indoors it can accumulate leading to elevated levels of Radon in the air resulting in health complications.

The Health Effects of Radon

Second only to smoking, Radon is the leading cause of lung cancer in the United States.

As Radon decays it releases small bursts of energy that can alter cells.

The problem is that when you inhale Radon into your lungs it continues to decay into finer particles that can implant themselves perfectly into the lungs.

This could potentially initiate cancer.

Exposure

Each year an estimated 21,000 deaths are linked to Radon according to the EPA.

How Radon Enters a Structure

As of January 1st, 2014 sellers are now required to provide buyers with a detailed disclosure.

The Office of the Surgeon General recommends that all homes be tested because it's the only way to tell if Radon levels are above a safe level.
